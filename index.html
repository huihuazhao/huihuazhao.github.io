About me
I received my B.S. and M.S. degrees in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Science and Technology of China in July 2010 and the Texas A&M University in May 2015, respectively. I transferred to Georgia Institute of Technology in May 2015 and finished my Ph.D. in August 2016. Now, I am a Control Research Engineer at Robotics | SRI International working on humanoid robots.

What do I do ?
I am interested in hybrid system, optimization, non-linear control and system integration for legged robots, with a special focus on both bipedal robots and lower-limb prostheses. My Ph.D. project is translating non-linear bipedal robotic control to realize human-like multi-contact stable prosthetic walking in 3D. Check this link for the recent work in AMBER Lab. More details can be found in my Ph.D. dissertation .
